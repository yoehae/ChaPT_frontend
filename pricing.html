Have questions? We're here to help.

Do I need a referral or prescription for physical therapy?

New York allows direct access to physical therapy — you can start treatment without a doctor's referral in most cases. However, some insurance plans may require a referral for coverage. Our team will verify this for you.

How many physical therapy visits does insurance typically cover?

Coverage varies widely. Many plans allow 20–60 visits per year, but some have limits based on condition or calendar year. We'll verify your specific benefits before treatment begins.

What if I'm out-of-network?

We happily see out-of-network patients and provide superbills for you to submit for reimbursement. Many patients find their out-of-network benefits make our care surprisingly affordable.

Does Medicare cover physical therapy?

Yes — Medicare Part B covers medically necessary outpatient physical therapy. There may be copays (typically 20% after deductible). We accept Medicare and will handle the billing.

Why might I still owe money with insurance?

Most plans include deductibles, copays per visit, or coinsurance. Once you reach plan limits or annual caps, you may be responsible for remaining costs. We provide estimates upfront so there are no surprises.

What if my claim is denied?

We'll help you understand the reason and assist with appeals or resubmissions when possible. Our billing team has experience navigating insurance challenges.
