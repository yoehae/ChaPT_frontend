Neurological Coordination

What Are Neurological Movement Disorders?

These are conditions caused by damage or dysfunction in the brain, spinal cord, or peripheral nerves, affecting how your body moves and responds.

Examples include:

  • Parkinson's Disease
  • Multiple Sclerosis (MS)
  • Post-Stroke Motor Deficits
  • Dystonia
  • Huntington's Disease
  • Cerebellar Ataxia
  • Functional Neurological Disorders (FND)

Common symptoms:

  • Tremors and shaky movements
  • Muscle stiffness or rigidity
  • Poor balance and posture
  • Difficulty starting or controlling movement
  • Fatigue with walking or standing

How We Treat It

At Cha Physical Therapy, we customize each treatment plan based on your condition, movement limitations, and personal goals. Our programs are grounded in neuroplasticity—helping your brain and nervous system relearn and rewire.

Our therapy may include:

  • Balance & Gait Training – Improve safety and walking ability
  • Task-Oriented Functional Training – Rebuild control in real-life movements
  • Neuromuscular Re-education – Strengthen the mind-body connection
  • Cueing & Feedback Techniques – Especially for Parkinson's gait
  • Posture & Core Stability Exercises – Improve alignment and endurance
  • Manual Therapy – Ease muscle tightness and joint stiffness
  • Spasticity & Tone Management – Use stretching, positioning, and modalities
  • Fall Prevention & Dual-Task Exercises – For everyday confidence

Your care is 100% personalized, with 1-on-1 sessions every visit.

Frequently Asked Questions about Neurological Physical Therapy:

Neurological movement disorders are conditions that affect the brain, spinal cord, or peripheral nerves, resulting in difficulty controlling movement and coordination. These disorders can range from degenerative conditions like Parkinson's Disease and Multiple Sclerosis to acquired conditions following a stroke. Each condition presents unique challenges and requires specialized rehabilitation approaches.

Common signs vary depending on the specific condition, but may include tremors and shaky movements, muscle stiffness or rigidity, poor balance and increased fall risk, difficulty initiating or controlling movement, slowness (bradykinesia), fatigue with physical activity, changes in posture and gait, and coordination problems. If you experience any of these symptoms, it's important to seek professional evaluation and specialized rehabilitation.

We use evidence-based approaches grounded in neuroplasticity to help your brain and nervous system relearn and rewire movement patterns. Our treatment includes balance and gait training, task-oriented functional training, neuromuscular re-education, cueing and feedback techniques, posture and core stability exercises, manual therapy, spasticity management, and fall prevention strategies. Every session is tailored to your specific condition and goals.

Personalization is at the heart of our approach. We conduct thorough assessments to understand your specific movement limitations, strengths, and personal goals. Rather than using cookie-cutter programs, each session is designed uniquely for you. You receive 1-on-1 attention from a skilled therapist who adjusts exercises and techniques based on your progress and changing needs. This individualized approach ensures you get the most effective rehabilitation for your specific condition.
